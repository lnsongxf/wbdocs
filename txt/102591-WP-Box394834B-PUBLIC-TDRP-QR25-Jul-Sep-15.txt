A F R I C A N    U N I O N    D D R    C A P A C I T Y    P R O G R A M    ( A U    D D R C P )                                                               
  
M e t h o d o l o g y    W o r k s h o p    o n    t h e    c o l l a b o r a t i v e    A s s e s s m e n t    o f    N o n    S t a t e    A r m e d   
G r o u p s   
  
From  13-­‐14  July  2015,  the  African  Union  Commission  (AUC)  convened  a  methodology  workshop  on  the  
collaborative  assessment  of  non-­‐state  armed  groups  in  Addis  Ababa,  Ethiopia.  The  meeting  was  organized  
as   a   follow-­‐up   from   the   main   outcomes   agreed   upon   from   the   4th   African   Union   Small   Arms   Light  
Weapons/Disarmament   Demobilization   and   Reintegration   (DDR)   Steering   Committee   Meeting   held   in  
Lusaka,   Zambia   from   8-­‐9   December   2014,   where   a   concept   paper   on   “Assessment   of   Non-­‐   Non   State  
Armed   Groups   (NSAGs)   and   Advancing   Stabilization,   DDR,   and   Cross   Border   Security   and   Development  
Efforts  on  the  African  Continent”  was  presented,  and  discussed.  
  
The  workshops  helped  the  AUC  and  its  partners  to  shape  the  analytical  guidelines,  provided  conceptual  
clarity   and   outlined   the   approach   and   methodology   in   the   concept   note   for   the   study.     A   detailed  
Operational  plan  of  the  study  was  also  presented,  and  the  Terms  of  Reference  (ToRs)  of  the  SALWs/DDR  
Steering  Committee  (SCM)  Task  Team/Sub-­‐Committee  serving  as  a  Critical  Review  Panel  for  the  execution  
of  the  study  was  discussed  as  well.    
  
These   intended   outcomes   will   support   the   final   review   and   adjustment   of   the   scope   of   the   study  
accordingly,   and   will   also   help   to   enhance   a   collaborative   and   coherent   engagement   strategy   by   all  
partners  interested  in  the  study  and  its  future  outcomes.  
  
D S D    F o l l o w -­‐    u p    A s s e s s m e n t    M i s s i o n    t o    M o g a d i s h u ,    S o m a l i a   
  
From   September   16-­‐17,   2015,   as   part   of   the   AU   Disarmament,   Demobilization   and   Reintegration   (DDR)  
Capacity   program,   the   Defense   and   Security   Division   (DSD)   undertook   an   assessment   mission   on  
Disarmament,   Demobilization   and   Reintegration   (DDR),   including   on   the   Reception   and   Hand-­‐Over   of  
Children  Separated  from  Armed  Groups  in  Somalia.  The  assessment  mission  included  four  critical  aspects  
of   security,   stabilization   and   development,   namely   (i)   Disarmament,   Demobilization   and   Reintegration  
(DDR)  including  arms  management,  (ii)  Security  Sector  Reform  (SSR),  (iii)  the  reception  and  hand-­‐over  of  
children  and  (iv)  women  separated  from  armed  groups.    
  
DSD  undertook  the  mission  to  provide  vital  DDR  support  to  AMISOM,  as  part  of  their  mandate  to  assist  in  
the  implementation  of  the  National  Security  Stabilization  Program  (NSSP).  The  mission  met  with  AMISOM,  
particularly   with   DDR/Disengaging   combatants,   Security   Sector   Reform   and   Gender   peace   and   Security  
officers,  United  Nations  Support  to  Somalia  (UNSOM)  and  the  Federal  Government  of  Somalia  (FGS).

1.2   C OUNTRY   A CTIVITIES   
  
C E N T R A L    A F R I C A N    R E P U B L I C   
	  
In   this   quarter,   TDRP   continued   working   with   the   United   Nations   Multidimensional   Integrated  
Stabilization  Mission  in  the  Central  African  Republic  (MINUSCA)  DDR  team  in  the  development  of  national  
DDR   program.   A   TDRP   mission   visited   Bangui   in   September   2015   and   met   with   officials   from   the  
Transitional  Government  who  are  working  on  DDR  and  Security  Sector  Reform,  World  Bank  country  team  
and   MINUSCA   leadership.   In   this   quarter,   TDPR   and   MINUSCA   DDR   developed   a   joint   proposal   for  
financing   support   from   the   UN-­‐World   Bank   Partnership   Trust   Fund,   which   aims   to   deepen   cooperation  
between  the  World  Bank  and  the  UN  in  fragile  and  conflict-­‐affected  situations.  TDRP  and  MINUSCA  DDR  
teams  will  develop  terms  of  reference  for  socio-­‐economic  assessment  of  beneficiaries  and  communities  
and   mapping   of   potential   implementing   partners.   TDRP   will   continue   supporting   MINUSCA   and   the  
Government  through  continued  coordination  and  technical  assistance  during  the  pre-­‐DDR  process.

On   July   09th   2015,   another   major   step   was   accomplished   with   the   signing   of   the   legal   documents  
(Financial  Agreement  &  Grant  Agreement)  of  the  Reinsertion  &  Reintegration  Project,  in  Kinshasa,  by  the  
Minister  of  Finance  and  the  World  Bank  Country  Director.  Following  the  signatures  of  the  legal  accords,  a  
World   Bank/TDRP   mission   travelled   to   Kinshasa   from   July   20   -­‐   30   with   the   objectives   to   start   the  
preparation  of  the  ESMF  (Environmental  Social  and  Management  Framework),  the  baseline  and  to  closely  
follow-­‐up  on  the  status  of  the  project  effectiveness.  During  the  mission  the  Sr.  Social  Safeguard  Specialist  
worked  with  the  UEPNDDR  to  prepare  the  required  ToRs  for  the  ESMF  and  also  conducted  a  training  on  
the  grievance  redress  mechanism  that  need  to  be  put  in  place.  The  mission,  which  included  the  WB  Sr.  
Financial   Management   Expert   and   Procurement   Specialist,   held   many   working   sessions   with   the  
UEPNDDR   to   advance   the   preparation   of   the   Project   Implementation   Manual.   In   addition,   the   mission  
received  updates  from  the  UEPNDDR  on  the  preliminary  activities  of  the  Project  related  to  disarmament  
and  demobilization  and  a  total  of  4,189  person  (including  89  female)  have  been  demobilized  at  the  end  of  
July   in   the   CTA   in   Kitona   and   Kamina.   Based   on   the  mission   recommendation,   the   UEPNDDR   finalized   a  
new  version  of  the  PIM,  and  a  last  set  of  comments  has  been  sent  to  the  UEPNDDR  to  have  a  final  version  
ready   by   early   October.   Regarding   the   effectiveness,   the   GoDRC   reviewed   the   signed   accords   during   a  
commission   and   it   is   expected   to   be   approved   during   a   Council   of   Minister   in   early   October   to   finally  
receive   the   legal   opinion   –   the   Project   should   therefore   be   effective   before   the   end   of   October.   In  
addition,   the   WB/TDRP   continued   to   follow   up   with   interested   donors   to   contribute   to   the   established  
MDTF,   and   a   new   contribution   from   the   EU   is   expected   for   the   month   of   October.   Finally,   a   WB/TDRP  
mission  went  to  Kinshasa  at  the  end  of  September/  early  October  to  continue  to  follow-­‐up  and  to  ensure  
that  activities  will  start  as  soon  as  possible  –  this  mission  will  be  reported  in  the  next  Quarterly  Report.

M A L I   
  
In  August,  the  World  Bank  Country  Management  Unit  requested  the  TDRP  team  to  develop  the  concept  
for   a   reinsertion   project.   As   an   integral   part   of   the   global   DDR   program,   the   project   would   provide  
reinsertion  support  to  disarmed  and  demobilized  ex-­‐combatants  through  a  community-­‐based  approach.  
It   is   expected   that   the   new   project   will   be   co-­‐financed   by   World   Bank’s   International   Development  
Association  (IDA)  and  a  new  multi-­‐donor  trust  fund  to  channel  donor  contributions.  In  October,  a  TDRP  
mission  will  travel  to  Bamako  to  discuss  the  reinsertion  project  concept  with  stakeholders  including  the  
Government,   World   Bank   country   team,   the   United   Nations   Multidimensional   Integrated   Stabilization  
Mission   in   Mali   (MINUSMA)   DDR   Unit   and   development   partners.   To   inform   the   design   of   reinsertion  
activities,   TDRP   has   been   working   with   a   local   research   institute   to   conduct   a   rapid   survey   of   armed  
groups.  In  addition,  TDRP  technical  support  continued  in  this  quarter,  focusing  on  organizational  set-­‐up  
and  institutional  framework  of  DDR.

S o u t h    S u d a n    D D R    T e c h n i c a l    A s s i s t a n c e   

This  quarter  the  Republic  of  South  Sudan  made  important  progress  when  on  August  27,  2015  President  
Kiir   signed   a   peace   agreement   with   the   opposition   forces,   officially   ending   the   conflict   which   has   been  
ongoing   since   December   2013.   While   there   are   still   challenges   in   terms   of   enforcement   and   additional  
work  to  be  done  to  fill  out  the  implementation  detail  for  the  agreement,  the  signing  of  the  agreement  is  a  
crucial  milestone  for  the  country.  The  TDRP  team  has  maintained  an  open  dialogue  with  the  South  Sudan  
National   DDR   Commission   (NDDRC)   throughout   this   crisis   particularly   regarding   the   country’s   DDR  
strategy  moving  forward  and  has  encouraged  the  NDDRC  to  advise  the  peace  talks  on  the  implementation  
details  of  the  agreement  so  as  to  avoid  some  of  the  pitfalls  which  followed  the  CPA  agreement.    The  TDRP  
team   is   also   in   the   process   of   briefing   new   World   Bank   country   team   management   on   these   issues   to  
continue  the  positive  momentum  developed  over  the  course  of  the  DDR  Pilot  Reintegration  Project.

The   TDRP   was   requested   to   share   best   practice   from   its   projects   with   the   Bangsamoro   Normalization  
(Reintegration)   Program   in   the   Philippines   and   the   ACR   (the   Colombian   Agency   for   Reintegration).

these   DDR   programs   were   interested   in   Institutional   arrangements   as   well   as   reintegration   modalities.

looks   at   strategies   employed   to   counter   both   trends   and   how   these   can   possibly   be   used   in   DDR
