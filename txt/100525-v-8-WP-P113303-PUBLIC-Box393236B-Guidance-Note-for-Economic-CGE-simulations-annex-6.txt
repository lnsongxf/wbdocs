     GUIDANCE NOTE FOR ECONOMIC EVALUATION OF
    CLIMATE CHANGE IMPACTS IN THE SAVA RIVER BASIN

1          Background
The objective of the economic analysis is to measure the magnitude and distributional costs of
climate change impacts and adaptation options under alternative water regime scenarios in the
Sava River Basin (Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Macedonia, Serbia and Slove-
nia). This analysis aims to capture climate change impact at the sector and economy-wide lev-
els. It builds on the main WATCAP report (World Bank, 2013b), the report on the development
of future climate scenarios (Vujadinovic and Vukovic, 2013); on the report on development of
the hydrologic model for the Sava River basin (World Bank, 2013a) presented as Annex 1 and
also for the Agriculture Guidance Note presented as Annex 5.



2          Framework
The Sava connects three South-European capitals: Ljubljana in Slovenia, Zagreb in Croatia
and Belgrade in Serbia (Figure 1). It is 945 km long and drains 95,719 km² of surface area.

In 2001, the riparian countries have launched a cooperation process known as “the Sava River
Basin Initiative”. The International Sava River Basin Commission (ISRBC)3 was found, in 2004,
to promote the regional cooperation on sustainable development, water resource utilization,
protection and management among Slovenia, Croatia, Serbia, Bosnia-Herzegovina, Montene-
gro and Albania.
This study adopts an integrated approach combining crop modelling with economy-wide analy-
sis:

   First, a methodology to estimate the change in crop production based on the changes in
    several climate variables was developed.
   Second, an economy-wide model for the SRB countries was developed to describe the
    most likely economic growth path for the regions without taking into account the potential
    impact of climate change.
   Third, the economy-wide model and the crop model were combined to better understanding
    of the impacts of climate change and adaptation options.

Hence different components developed in this analysis where:

   Economic baseline refer to the most likely growth path that the SRB countries are expected
    to follow based on the published forecasts from the World Bank, IMF and USDA studies.
   Climate scenarios are developed on the basis of various trends regarding the pace and
    amplitude of Climate Change impact on the SRB.
   Crop modeling is based on historic climate variables and climate projections.
   Adaptation Plan (AP) recommendations on various potential adaptation policies are devel-
    oped using the CGE model on the basis of the climate and crop models’ assumptions.

The analytical methods and data used in this analysis aim to provide a basis for the preparation
of guidance notes on the economic analysis of climate change and Adaptation Plans (AP)
which are also planned to be incorporated into the Water and Climate Change projects in ECA.



3         Description of the Sector/economy-wide level analysis
The economic analysis focuses on the macro-level impacts of climate change through country
and inter-regional computable general equilibrium analyses (CGE)3. 30 years continuous data
for selected climate variables was collected.

The results from 5 GCM model analyses are introduced into the CGE model. Overall impact on
SRB countries’ GDP, consumption and agricultural productions were estimated for the next 55+
years.



4         Drivers of economic growth
Data was retrieved from the IMF World Economic Outlook database for the SRB countries: Al-
bania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Macedonia, Serbia and Slovenia. The database used
in the CGE modelling (GTAP) has 2007 as the reference year, the simulation exercises are
conducted over the period 2010- 2070. This paper presents (i) available published statistics for
the period 2007-2013, (ii) how they are used as inputs for the model simulations, and (iii) pre-
liminary simulations results till 2070.

Two types of data are at the origin of our forecasts: Actual (observed) growth rates in 2007-
2013 and IMF and USDA growth estimates for 2014-2070 for each SRB country.

   GDP per capita: GDP in national currency is converted to current U.S. dollars and divided
    by the total population.
   Population (persons).

First, GDP estimates are derived by multiplying the GDP in U.S. dollars by total population,
then, annual growth rates are computed and expressed in percentage changes.

GDP growth estimator is expressed in current USD; therefore it projects the potential economic
expansion (or contraction) in the SRB economies, and also includes the changes in the curren-
cies vis a vis USD (constant, 2007).

In these estimations, the population growth intervenes as one of the drivers of economic ex-
pansion (a raise in population would mean an increase in the labour force hence in the coun-
try’s economic performance).

The SRB countries analysed in this study represent in total 23 million people. Serbia is the
most and Slovenia is the less populated country (with respectively 7.5 million and 2 million
people). According to the United Nations’ forecast, in the next six years, Albania and Macedo-
nia’s populations are expected to grow by around 3%, Bosnia- Herzegovina and Serbia by
around 1%, while Croatia’s and Serbia’s populations are expected to stay almost constant
(Figure 2).

6             Baseline CGE: (2010-2070)
Baseline refers to the most likely growth path that the SRB countries are expected to follow and
does not take into account any potential climate change impact. The Baseline is used as the
reference scenario against which the climate scenarios will be compared.

Model Baseline simulations consist of using the CGE model to replicate the SRB countries’
growth performances predicted by the IMF for 2004-2015, these projections are extended to
2070 using information from different sources (e.g. USDA). This exercise comprises three
steps:

      First, population growth forecast are used as an inputs; shocks corresponding to the annual
       population growth are applied to the base year population data for each country represent-
       ed in the GTAP database (e.g. 0.55% for Albania, -0.07% for Croatia—see Table 2 in the
       Annex for details),
      Second, the Total Factor Productivity (TFP) growth estimates (from the literature) are ap-
       plied as a shock to the initial model parameters,
      Finally, the capital accumulation parameter is adjusted in order to reach the GDP growth
       estimates developed by the IMF and USDA.

7        Simulations design within the CGE (2010-2070): CLM,
         HadRM3Q0, RACMO, REMO RegCM3
This study adapted a multidisciplinary approach. Different modeling techniques are combined
to provide a robust effort in understanding the impact of Climate Change on Water Resources
in SRB. Based on historic trends; future projections and various scenarios have been devel-
oped with regard to temperature and precipitation.

On the basis of technical analyses of historic meteorological and hydrological data trends,
General 5 Circulation Model (GCM) projections CLM, HadRM3Q0, RACMO, REMO RegCM3.
Using the WATCAP model, Climate Change impact is translated into yield changes for each
crop produced in different SRB countries in the horizon to 2040 and 2070.

These yield changes are introduced as productivity (TFP) shocks into the CGE model. These
generate a ripple effect through price changes which will be reflected to the market prices of
analyzed crops which down the line will have an impact on consumer welfare, external trade
and growth.



8        CGE simulations on Climate Change impact
Countries facing a severe impact of climate change on the agricultural sector will witness rising
agricultural prices which will be reflected to higher consumer prices. Rising prices will negative-
ly affect consumer’s disposable income and incentivize them to substitute the consumption of
agricultural goods with less expensive commodities or imported agricultural products.

Simulations results on potential price changes with respect to their value in 2007 is given for
each crop in the Annex: these are estimated to reach 6% for winter wheat, 25% for maize, 25%
for potato &tomato &grape, 10% for sunflower, 19% for sugar beet in various years.

Regarding production of crops (see Annex); they are estimated to increase between 12 to 25 –
fold in 2070 compared to their levels in 2007 (in constant prices, USD).
