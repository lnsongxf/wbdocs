A F R I C A N    U N I O N    D D R    C A P A C I T Y    P R O G R A M    ( A U    D D R C P )                                   
During   the   third   quarter   of   FY15,   as   per   the   agreed   task-­‐team   recommendations   with   the   DDR/SALW  
steering   committee   AU   DDRCP   has   developed   the   documentations   required   to   conduct   the   Non-­‐   State  
Armed   Groups   study,   including   terms   of   reference,   draft   operational   plan   is   prepared   to   undertake   the  
study  on  Non  State  Armed  groups,  and  a  concept  note  that  clearly  outlines  the  objectives  and  outcomes  
of  the  upcoming  workshop  on  NSAGs.  
Further,   analytical   framework   and   literature   review   that   guides   the   development   of   the   AU   DDR  
compendium   has   also   been  prepared.   The   document   is   expected   to   go   through   further   consultation   by  
the  Centers  of  Excellence,  RECs  and  selected  Member  States  in  order  to  finalize  the  selection  of  the  case  
studies  and  the  particular  themes  that  will  be  explored  in  each  of  those  studies.  A  workshop  is  scheduled  
to  be  held  from  12-­‐13  May  2015  to  receive  guidance  and  input  on  the  direction  of  the  case  studies  and  
approach  to  be  taken  as  well  as  gather  information  from  the  various  DDR  training  entities  on  the  current  
DDR  training  offerings  and  approach  utilized.  
In   terms   of   AU   DDR   Guidelines,   foreign   fighters   and   HIV/AIDS   components   have   been   underway,   and   a  
consultant   position   is   advertised   to   develop   the   DDR   guideline   on   Countering   Violent   Extremism   (CVE)  
and  de-­‐radicalisation.    
In   addition,   the   AU   DDR   website   is   released   online   on   February   2015.   The   website   will   eventually  
integrate   in   to   the   Peace   and   Security   Department’s   website.   Besides,   the   AU   DDR   Newsletter   third  
edition  is  released  on  February  2015.

Com m unity  Reintegration  Project:  Im plem entation  Com pletion  and  Results  (ICR)  
  
In  March  2015,  TDRP  began  to  prepare  the  Implementation  Completion  and  Results  (ICR)  report  for  the  
CAR:   Community   Reintegration   Project   (CRP).   CRP   was   financed   by   the   TDRP   Trust   Fund   with   the   long-­‐
term  objective  of  supporting  the  peace  process  and  contribute  to  Government’s  stabilization  efforts,  the  
project   was   developed   as   a   community   recovery   project   in   war-­‐affected   area   of   northern   CAR,  
particularly  in  those  areas  where  there  is  a  high  concentration  of  ex-­‐combatants,  youth  associated  with  
militia   and   banditry,   and   youth-­‐at-­‐risk.   CRP   attempted   to   bridge   the   gap   from   the   traditional  
demobilization  and  reintegration  operation  to  longer  term  reintegration  and  recovery.  The  TDRP  team  is  
currently  conducting  an  Implementation  Completion  and  Results  (ICR)  mission  in  Bangui,  CAR  until  early  
May   2015   to   collect   results   data,   carry   out   interviews   and   implement   a   stakeholder   workshop   with   the  
four   implementing   partners,   namely   ACTED,   Solidarité,   Première   Urgence,   and   International   Rescue  
Committee  as  well  as  other  key  stakeholders  including  the  Government.  In  addition,  IRC  has  also  agreed

The   TDRP   Team   continued   to   work   on   the   preparation   of   the   DDRIII   Program   for   the   DRC   during   this  
quarter  and  focused  on  the  preparation  of  the  Project  Appraisal  Document  for  the  World  Bank.  A  major  
milestone  of  the  internal  processing  of  the  Project  was  reached  with  the  Decision  Meeting  (DM)  that  took  
place   on   February,   20th   2015.   The   Country   Director   for   the   DRC   chaired   the   meeting   and   provided   his  
clearance  to  the  Team  to  move  forward  and  undertake  the  appraisal  and  the  negotiations  of  the  Project.  
As  a  result  of  the  DM,  it  was  decided  to  design  the  Project  with  the  current  financing  available,  namely  
the  US$15  million  provided  by  IDA  and  the  US$6.5  million  made  available  by  Sweden  through  the  TDRP.  
A  TDRP  mission  went  to  Kinshasa  immediately  following  the  Decision  Meeting  (February  25  –  March  05)  
to   discuss   with   the   Congolese   counterpart   and   address   jointly   the   comments   provided   by   the   Country  
Director  as  well  as  different  experts  from  the  World  Bank.  The  mission  successfully  updated  the  Project  
Document   with   the   Congolese   counterparts   and   re-­‐designed   the   Project   to   address   the   first   caseload  
(4,800  ex-­‐combatants  waiting  in  the  demobilization  camps)  for  a  total  of  $21.5  million.  The  mission’s  next  
step  has  been  to  prepare  the  ground  for  the  appraisal  and  negotiations  mission  which  included  agreeing  
on   a   date   with   the   Ministry   of   Finance   as   well   as   finalizing   the   legal   documents   (Grant   agreement,  
disbursement  letter,  etc.).  
The   month   of   March   was   used   to   focus   on   finalizing   the   Project   Document   and   obtain   the   necessary  
clearances   (Chief   Legal   Counsel,   Procurement   Specialist,   Finance   Management   Specialist,   and  
Disbursement   Officer)   within   the   World   Bank   to   be   able   to   negotiate   with   the   Government   of   DRC.   A  
mission   went   back   to   Kinshasa   at   the   end   of   March   to   undertake   the   appraisal   of   the   Project   directly  
followed   by   the   negotiations   with   the   Government.   This   mission   will   be   described   in   the   next   quarterly  
report.  
While   working   on   the   Project   Document,   the   TRDP   also   worked   on   preparing   the   establishment   of   the  
Multi-­‐Donor  Trust  Fund  (MDTF)  that  will  received  the  additional  funds  from  other  potential  donors.  Both  
DFID  and  the  European  Commission  have  expressed  a  strong  interest  in  supporting  the  DDR  IIII  operation.  
The  TDRP  team  worked  with  the  WB  colleagues  on  the  preparation  of  the  Administrative  Agreement  that

Group  
  
In   January   2015,   the   Government   of   Mali   requested   TDRP   participation   in   a   DDR   Seminar   organized   by  
the  Government  of  Mali.  The  Seminar  was  scheduled  to  take  place  on  January  12,  2015  with  the  objective  
of   sensitizing   relevant   ministries   on   the   National   DDR   Strategy   and   planned   DDR   operations.   Due   to  
resignation   of   the   Prime   Minister   on   January   09,   the   DDR   Seminar   was   canceled.   The   Government,  
however,   requested   the   mission   as   planned   for   meetings   with   the   Ministry   of   Defense   and   the   DDR  
Working   Group.   The   mission   identified   the   priority   areas   for   continued   technical   assistance,   namely  
financing  mechanisms,  and  institutional  framework.  In  addition,  TDRP  was  recommended  to  undertake  a  
preliminary  study  of  armed  groups  that  are  participating  in  the  peace  talks.  In  this  context,  the  mission  
met  with  two  Malian  experts  in  Bamako  for  a  future  focus  groups  exercise.  The  objective  of  this  activity  
would   be   to   prepare   a   standard   briefing   note   showing   membership   dynamics   and   socio-­‐economic  
reintegration   aspects   on   each   group.   In   March   2015,   the   TDRP   team   and   the   DDR   Working   Group   also  
prepared  a  draft  institutional  framework  that  could  be  adapted  once  a  final  peace  agreement  is  reached  
between  the  parties.  
  
The  TDRP  has  met  with  TTLs  from  other  Bank  operations  in  Mali  to  identify  synergies  in  preparation  of  a  
potential  DDR  project  once  the  environment  is  conducive  for  such  a  project.  The  approach  was  to  identify  
areas  of  Bank  projects  that  have  interventions/components  where  ex-­‐combatants  could  benefit  from  in  
terms   of   livelihood   support   and   vocational   and   skills   training.   By   doing   this,   the   DDR   project   would  
leverage   funds   and   aim   to   ensure   a   more   sustainable   reintegration   support   for   its   target   groups.   Key  
potential   synergies   were   identified   with   the   Skills   Development   and   Youth   Employment   Project,  
Emergency  Safety  Net  Project,  and  the  Mali  Reconstruction  and  Economic  Recovery  Project.

South  Sudan  DDR  Technical  Assistance  
  
Following   on   the   completion   of   the   South   Sudan   DDR   Pilot   in   November   2014,   this   quarter   the   TDRP  
worked  with  the  South  Sudan  National  DDR  Commission  (NDDRC)  to  build  an  updated  DDR  strategy  given  
the  current  realities.    The  draft  “Republic  of  South  Sudan  National  DDR  Strategy:  Harmonized  Reinsertion  
and  Reintegration  Programming  Analytical  Paper  to  inform  next  National  DDR  Strategy  for  RoSS”  presents  
an  analysis  of  the  key  groups  and  reintegration  programme  options  to  be  considered  in  the  context  of  a  
full  and  comprehensive  Disarmament,  Demobilisation  and  Reintegration  (DDR)  program  for  the  Republic  
Of   South   Sudan   (RoSS).   It   identifies   key   strategic   or   programmatic   developments   needed   in   order   to  
create  an  environment  where  DDR  reintegration  programming  can  be  successful  including  Security  Sector  
Reform   (SSR)   and   establishment   of   Veterans   Affairs   (VA)   and   Military   Pensions.   The   paper   builds   on  
learning   from   the   prior   phases   of   DDR   in   RoSS   including   the   CPA   DDR   and   the   2013-­‐2014   DDR   Pilot  
Program.   The   document   takes   into   account   the   changed   circumstances   in   RoSS   since   the   outbreak   of  
conflict  on  December  14th,  2013.  It  also  takes  into  account  the  fact  that  while  the  country  context  has  
changed  this  can  present  an  opportunity  to  reset  some  ways  of  working  in  DDR  that  negatively  affect  the  
impact,  efficiency  and  effectiveness  of  reintegration  programming.    
DDR   was   also   highlighted   by   the   Government   of   South   Sudan   delegation   at   the   World   Bank   Spring  
Meetings  held  in  April  2015  as  a  priority  area  for  the  government  when  looking  to  stabilization,  recovery  
and  development.  The  Minister  of  Finance  outlined  the  need  for  DDR  as  both  a  fiscal  and  developmental  
concern  and  this  priority  was  reflected  by  the  World  Bank  country  management.    The  DDR  Chairperson  
was  asked  by  the  Government  to  join  this  year’s  delegation  and  the  TDRP  team  met  with  the  Chairperson  
several  times  over  the  course  of  the  Spring  Meetings.    
  
Japan   Social   Development   Fund   (JSDF):   Economic   Empowerment   Training   for   Conflict-­‐
Affected  Youth  Project  
  
The   Project   Concept   Note   for   the   South   Sudan   Economic   Empowerment   Training   for   Conflict-­‐Affected  
Youth  Project  was  completed  this  quarter  and  will  be    presented  at  a  Project  Concept  Review  on  May  20,  
2015.      The   project   aims   to   provide   an   economic   empowerment   training   program   for   conflict-­‐affected  
youth  in  South  Sudan  through  development  of  the  curriculum,  skilling  of  trainers  and  piloting  the  training.  
This   is   a   critical   initiative   as   it   will   enhance   the   country’s   training   capacity   which   will   be   necessary   for  
eventual   implementation   of   any   DDR   program   in   the   country.   At   the   same   time   there   is   widespread  
agreement  that  livelihoods  support  for  youth  is  a  priority  in  South  Sudan.  Unfortunately  the  livelihoods  
models  primarily  rely  on  traditional  vocational  training  or  TVET  which  has  been  found  to  be  inaccessible  
to   conflict-­‐affected   youth   and   not   targeted   for   their   needs.   The   proposed   project   aims   to   redesign   the  
approach  taken  in  traditional  livelihoods  programming  to  provide  more  realistic  and  targeted  training  for  
conflict-­‐affected   youth.   This   project   will   develop   the   tools   and   trainers   for   a   holistic   suite   of   economic  
empowerment   training   specifically   adapted   to   the   needs   of   conflict-­‐affected   youth   and   pilot   this   new  
approach   through   a   set   of   trainings   for   conflict-­‐affected   youth.   The   programming   will   be   designed  
specifically   for   this   vulnerable   group,   with   particular   recognition   of   low   literacy/educational   attainment  
levels,   challenges   resulting   from   exposure   to   traumatic   experiences,   and   time-­‐use   cost-­‐benefit   realities.  
Traditional   vocational   training   and   TVET   programming   has   been   found   to   be   largely   ineffective   as   it  
presupposes   levels   of   literacy,   employment   opportunities   and   long   timelines   of   commitment   which   are  
not  realistic  for  these  youth.  The  innovative  approach  being  piloted  here  instead  builds  skills  that  allow  
for  immediate  use  in  their  communities  while  training  for  long-­‐term  economic  flexibility  and  mobility.

2 .    P R O G R A M    M A N A G E M E N T               
TFC    M EETING                          
  
The  TDRP  Trust  Fund  Committee  (TFC)  held  its  regular  meeting  in  Paris,  France  on  February  4  and  5,  2015.  
The  African  Development  Bank  (AfDB),  Finland,  Norway,  Belgium,  and  Sweden  of  the  eight  TDRP  donors  
attended   the   meeting,   which   was   chaired   by   Abderrahim   Fraiji,   TDRP   Manager.   The   TDRP   Manager  
presented   the   ongoing   technical   assistance   activities   as   well   as   plans   for   activities   under   a   proposed   4-­‐
year  TDRP  continuation.  
During   the   discussion,   the   TDRP   TFC   members   expressed   their   continued   support   to   the   TDRP,   though  
written   commitments/pledges   need   to   be   discussed   with   their   respective   departments.   TDRP   team  
agreed   to   present   the   donors   with   a   detailed   proposal,   elaborating   the   rationale   behind   the   TDRP  
extension.   What   was   made   clear   in   terms   of   moving   forward   was   that   the   4-­‐year   cost   extension   would  
not   include   investments   in   operations   but   would   be   primarily   designed   to   use   the   TDRP   as   a   vehicle   to

TDRP    E XTENSION   
  
The  TDRP  team  has  been  in  close  contact  with  the  TDRP  Trust  Fund  donors  to  initiate  the  process  of  a  no-­‐
cost   extension.   Donors   have   received   the   Amendment   Letters   to   the   Administration   Agreement   which  
stipulates   that   the   TDRP   MDTF   shall   be   extended   from   December   31,   2015   to   December   31,   2016.   A  
further  cost  extension  will  be  processed  once  written  pledges  of  additional  financial  contributions  to  the  
MDTF  will  be  made  by  donors.
