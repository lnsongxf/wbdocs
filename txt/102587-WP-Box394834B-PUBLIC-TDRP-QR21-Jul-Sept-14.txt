TDRP  has  been  asked  to  provide  technical  assistance  to  the  Government  of  Democratic  Republic  of  Congo  
DDR  Phase  III.  In  this  quarter,  TDRP’s  work  focused  on  two  major  activities  as  part  of  DDR  III  in  DRC:  (i)  
preparation   of   a   World   Bank   International   Development   Agency   (IDA)   financed   project   and   (ii)   the  
establishment  of  a  new  multi-­‐donor  trust  fund  through  which  donor  contributions  to  the  new  phase  of  
DDR   can   be   channeled.   It   is   expected   that   the   overall   DDR   III   Program   will   cost   USD$85   million.   The  
Government,  United  Nations  Organization  Stabilization  Mission  in  the  DR  Congo  (MONUSCO),  a  number  
of  donors,  and  the  World  Bank  will  finance  the  program.    
  
M A L I   
  
As  part  of  TDRP’s  technical  assistance  to  the  Government  of  Mali  DDR  Working  Group  (DDRWG),  a  TDRP  
team  supported  DDRWG  in  preparing  Mali’s  draft  Strategic  DDR  Plan.  The  plan,  which  was  finalized  in  July,  
consists   of   10   DDR   documents:   (1)   Reinsertion   &   Reintegration   Programming,   (2)   Communication   and  
Public   Information,   (3)   Management   Information   System,   (4)   Monitoring   &   Evaluation,   (5)   Funding  
Mechanisms,  (6)  Children  Associated  with  Armed  Forces,  (7)  Women  Associated  with  Armed  Forces,  (8)  
Disability   and   Psycho-­‐social   Programming,   (9)   Pre-­‐DDR   Activities,   and   (10)   Key   Stakeholders.   In   this  
quarter,  the  TDRP  team  has  started  working  on  the  DDR  Operations  Plan  together  with  the  DDR  Working  
Group.    
  
S O M A L I A   
  
TDRP  continued  to  work  on  the  South-­‐South  Knowledge  and  Experience  Exchange  initiative,  together  with  
the   African   Union,   between   Rwanda   and   Somalia.   It   is   expected   that   Somalia   DDR   team   will   obtain   key  
insight  into  DDR  affairs  by  interacting  with  their  Rwandese  counterparts.  Rwanda’s  experience  with  DDR  
is  widely  considered  as  one  of  the  best  in  Africa.  The  South-­‐South  Exchange  plan  involves  the  Somali  DDR  
department  staff  visiting  Kigali  to  meet  with  Rwanda  DDR  national  program  officials.  The  exchange  will  be  
in  the  form  of  discussions,  presentations,  working  groups  and  site  visits.  Rwanda  DDR  commission  is  very  
interested   to   receive   the   Somali   colleagues   and   share   with   them   its   DDR   experience.   TDRP   is   working  
closely  with  the  World  Bank’s  South-­‐South  Knowledge  Exchange  Facility,  which  is  providing  financing  to  
TDRP  for  implementing  an  effective  exchange  and  learning  activity.  It  is  expected  that  the  exchange  will  
be  in  the  form  of  a  5-­‐day  workshop,  including  site  visits  to  DDR  facilities,  in  November  2014.  
  
R E P U B L I C    O F    S O U T H    S U D A N                        
  
D D R    P i l o t    R e i n t e g r a t i o n    P r o j e c t   
  
The  TDRP  has  been  working  closely  with  the  Republic  of  South  Sudan’s  National  DDR  Commission  (NDDRC)  
since   2011   in   the   design,   piloting   and   incorporation   of   the   lessons   learned   into   the   country’s   new   DDR  
program.   This   new   program   aims   to   enhance   the   country’s   stability   and   prosperity   by   facilitating   the  
return  to  productive  civilian  life  of  current  members  of  the  National  Organized  Forces.  It  was  determined  
in   2011   by   the   government   to   start   the   Program   with   a   pilot,   during   which   lessons   learned   could   be

incorporated   before   scaling   up   to   the   full   caseload.   The   primary   objective   of   the   NDDRC’s   Pilot  
Reintegration  Project  was  to  test  reintegration  approaches  and  modalities  and  develop  actionable  lessons  
to  inform  and  improve  the  Republic  of  South  Sudan’s  future  DDR  programming.    At  the  same  time,  the  
pilot  was  to  develop  tools  for  use  in  future  DDR  tranches  and  enhance  the  capacity  of  NDDRC  staff  and  
systems.  
While  the  current  events  necessitate  attention  to  emergency  needs,  the  conflict  only  further  underscores  
the   importance   to   the   country   and   the   nation’s   overall   recovery   and   development   of   disarming,  
demobilizing  and  peacefully  reintegrating  former  soldiers  to  rejoin  civilian  life.  The  TDRP  completed  the  
Pilot  Reintegration  Project  as  initiated  in  April  2013  with  the  aim  of  facilitating  this  progress  in  this  critical  
time  
Specifically   this   quarter,   the   TDRP   focused   on   completing   the   service   delivery   of   the   pilot   reintegration  
project,   on   providing   capacity   building   to   the   NDDRC   on   M&E   and   Information   Counseling   and   Referral  
Systems  (ICRS)  and  on  compilation  of  lessons  learned.      
In  July  2014  a  TDRP  mission  team  visited  the  Republic  of  South  Sudan  from  July  13-­‐July  26,  2014  during  
which   the   team   provided   technical   assistance   to   the   NDDRC   in   operationalizing   their   Monitoring   and  
Evaluation   Department   while   also   training   and   assisting   the   NDDRC   to   undertake   an   ex-­‐combatant  
satisfaction  survey.  Technical  assistance  was  provided  to  the  NDDRC  in  operationalizing  their  Monitoring  
and  Evaluation  Department  through  a  South-­‐South  Knowledge  Exchange  conducted  by  the  coordinator  of  
the   Rwandan   DDR   Commission.   The   purpose   of   the   exchange   was   to   contribute   to   capacity   building  
especially   in   Monitoring   and   Evaluation,   as   well   as   advice   on   Information,   Counseling   and   Referral  
Systems  (ICRS).    During  and  following  the  mission,  the  consultant  completed  a  Monitoring  and  Evaluation  
manual.  The  manual  provides  step-­‐by  step  guidance  on  monitoring  and  evaluation  specifically  designed  
for  the  South  Sudan  NDDRC.  The  manual  also  provides  guidance  in  collecting  data  that  will  feed  into  the  
newly  developed  ICRS  software  and  database.    
Secondly,  the  mission  provided  technical  training  to  the  Information  Counseling  and  Referral  System  (ICRS)  
caseworkers   and   assistance   to   the   NDDRC   to   undertake   an   ex-­‐combatant   satisfaction   survey.     This  
included   a   one   day   training   conducted   at   the   World   Bank   Juba   office   to   i)   review   the   survey  
questionnaires,   ii)   incorporate   any   recommendations   based   on   caseworker   field-­‐knowledge   and   iii)  
demonstrate   and   practice   the   actual   process   of   conducting   the   tablet-­‐based   survey.   In   advance   of   the  
training,   the   Ex-­‐Combatant   Satisfaction   Survey   questionnaire   was   drafted   and   then   programmed   and  
tested   on   tablets.   ICRS   caseworkers   were   deployed   to   the   four   participating   states   in   August   2014,  
collecting  information  on  a  total  of  204  participating  ex-­‐combatants  (just  over  the  70%  mark).  The  data  
was   compiled   and   a   TDRP   consultant   completed   the   Ex-­‐Combatant   Satisfaction   Survey   report   in  
September  2014  (available  at  www.TDRP.net)    
Throughout   this   quarter   the   TDRP   team   also   provided   management   oversight   to   the   NDDRC   Pilot  
Reintegration   Project   including   during   the   mission   when   conducting   meetings   with   implementing  
partners  and  discussing  with  the  NDDRC  details  for  the  final  independent  evaluation  of  the  pilot  project.  
This   included   meeting   with   implementing   partners   on   Livelihoods   (UNICON   International)   and   on   DDR  
Community  Projects  (IOM)  as  the  firms  completed  their  service  delivery  and  reporting.    IOM  completed  
their   work   in   May,   having   completed   17   community   projects   on   time   (an   additional   5,   at   no   additional  
cost,  to  the  initially  contracted  12  projects).  UNICON  continued  their  livelihoods  support  service  delivery  
work  until  July  to  account  for  the  break  in  services  due  to  the  civil  strife  from  January  to  May.  UNICON

training   of   trainers   of   national   staff   including   ICRS   caseworkers   and   Ministry   of   Agriculture   Extension  
Workers.  The  mission  team  additionally  held  meetings  with  the  NDDRC  leadership  and  with  the  German

Also  this  quarter,  TDRP  has  continued  the  momentum  on  the  Assessment  of  Crime  and  Criminal  Violence  
Prevention   in   Policy   and   Decision   Making   in   South   Sudan   which   was   requested   by   the   South   Sudan  
National  DDR  Commission.  The  overarching  objectives  of  the  assessment  was  to  uncover  the  status  quo  
of  crime  and  violence  and  map  out  potential  areas  at  the  policy  level  for  integrated  approaches  towards  
crime  and  criminal  violence.  Of  particular  concern  was  the  role  that  ex-­‐combatants  play  in  this  area,  both  
as  perpetrators  and  survivors  of  crime  and  violence.

During   the   July   mission,   the   TDRP   built   upon   the   fieldwork   for   the   Assessment   of   Crime   and   Criminal  
Violence   Prevention   in   Policy   and   Decision   Making   conducted   in   June   by   piloting   a   Juba   Victimization  
Survey.   This   objective   was   met   through   the   following   steps:   1)   Trainings   to   local   consultants   on  
conducting   the   victimization   survey,   mapping   Enumerator   Areas   (EA)   and   operating   tablets.   2)   EA  
mapping  for  victimization  survey  piloting.  3)  Victimization  survey  piloting  in  three  randomly  chosen  EAs.  
The  draft  victimization  survey  questionnaire  was  programmed  and  tested  on  the  tablets.  Adjustments  and  
modifications   were   made   before   local   consultants   were   deployed   to   assigned   EAs   on   July   23rd.   In   total,  
126  questionnaires  were  completed  and  submitted  by  August  4th  for  data  analysis.  The  pilot  victimization  
survey  data  was  analyzed  and  included  in  the  overall  report.    
The  South  Sudan  Crime  and  Violence  Assessment  Report  released  in  October  2014  includes  the  following:  
      •       Presents  a  brief  overview  of  the  recent  history  of  South  Sudan,  and  describing  the  issues  related  
              to  crime  and  violence  that  are  examined  in  the  report.    
      •       Reviews  some  of  the  main  risk  factors  that  can  influence  current  and  future  levels  of  crime  and  
              violence  in  South  Sudan.    
      •       Presents,  estimates  and  discusses  the  socioeconomic  costs  of  violence  in  South  Sudan.    
      •       Discusses  the  institutional  settings  and  the  performance  of  critical  institutions,  such  as  the  police,  
              in  combating  crime.    
      •       Presents  and  discusses  the  results  of  a  pilot  victimization  survey  conducted  in  Juba,  South  Sudan.    
      •       Presents  the  conclusions  of  this  work  and  presents  a  suggested  road  map  for  the  World  Bank’s  
              future  engagement  in  this  sector  in  South  Sudan.

I N T E R -­‐ A G E N C Y    W O R K I N G    G R O U P    O N    D D R                                 
  
TDRP   continues   to   be   very   involved   in   the   United   Nations   Inter-­‐Agency   Working   Group   (IAWG)   on  
disarmament,   demobilization   and   reintegration   (DDR).   The   group   is   co-­‐chaired   by   the   DDR   units   of   the  
Department  of  Peacekeeping  Operations  (DPKO)  and  the  United  Nations  Development  Program  (UNDP).  
TDRP’s   participation   reflects   the   importance   and   emphasis   the   World   Bank,   AFTCS   and   TDRP   places   on  
coordination,  best  practices  and  information  sharing  among  partners.  This  quarter  the  TDRP  participated  
in  the  multi-­‐organizational  high-­‐level  meeting  held  in  April  2014  in  New  York  to  reaffirm  the  international  
commitment  and  approve  the  updated  work  plan.  As  part  of  this  renewed  work,  TDRP  will  be  co-­‐leading  
the   IAWG’s   development   of   IDDRS   modules   on   Communications,   Monitoring   &   Evaluation   and   Dealing  
with  Third  Country  nationals  (mercenaries)  in  DDR  Operations.
